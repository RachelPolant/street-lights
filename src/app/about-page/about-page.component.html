The Genesis of Street Lights


We live in a world where feeling safe isn’t created equally. Only 6% of individuals who experience harassment file a formal complaint. That means on average, 94% of victims don’t report it, and when they do, most establishments have the power to make the incident disappear.


While traveling, Raeanna Bektashi found herself out at local bar in Bali, Indonesia. She noticed a single woman who seemed overly intoxicated to a point where she was incapacitated. A local male kept bringing her more drinks and grasping her inappropriately. While he was at the bar, Rae approached the woman if she was okay and if she wanted to be around him. She pleaded by saying, "No," and just wanted to find her friends. While helping her, the man returned. Raeanna intervened and told him the woman did not want his attention. He then became aggressive and angry. The woman's friends rescued her from the situation, and Raeanna then left the bar. The next day, while talking to a friend who lives in the area about the situation, he stated, "Why would you go to that bar? Girls get raped there all the time!" The fact that a horrific incident like rape can happen not only once, but recurrently to the point where it creates a reputation is unacceptable.


We wanted to shed light on this issue. Street Lights is an app that allows users to find safe and inviting spaces, as well as anonymously report incidents that might make a place less inviting.


So, please join us in using Street Lights to help build a community dedicated to cultivating a safe space for everyone, every where.


Raeanna Bektashi, Rachel Polant, Joseph Cambridge, and Tony French are proud to bring you this application. It is our hope to help create safe and welcoming spaces for women, LGBTQA members, and all minorites who have felt threated and ignored.
